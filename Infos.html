Useful Tips

Coronado Island

The island is just minutes away from downtown San Diego. There are two ways of reaching it such as Coronado Ferry or Iconic Coronado Bridge. Apart from driving, you can walk or bike while exploring the peaceful island.


Hotel Del Coronado

It is the Victorian architectural masterpiece which was built in 1888. The hotel provides beautiful ocean view, shiny sandy beach with palms, dining near to the ocean and many more.


Coronado Ferry Landing Pier

By using Flagship Ferry, you can get across the San Diego Bay to Downtown San Diego. The Flagship cruises provide harbor tours and whale watching tours as well. tickets and further information can be found at the link provided below.


Coronado Beaches

  • Silver Strand State Beach
  • Glorietta Bay Beach
  • North Beach
  • Coronado Central Beach
  • Coronado Shores Beach/South Beach

Note: The North Beach is a dog-friendly beach.


Downtown and Neighborhoods

Downtown San Diego provides different arts, cultural events, dynamic dining and entertainments. Different neighborhoods are listed below which should be visited before leaving San Diego.

  • Gaslamp Quarter
  • Hillcrest
  • Little Italy
  • North Park
  • South Park

Gaslamp Quarter

It is historic heart of San Diego. You can enjoy galleries, rooftop bars, craft beer haunts and different dining places as well as nightclubs. What's happening at the heart of San Diego? Here is a list of popular places ;

  • Petco Park
  • The Lady Shell at Jacobs Park
  • USS Midway Museum
  • Sky-high rooftop Bars
  • Seaport Village
  • Maritime Museum
